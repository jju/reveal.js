publicity & privacy

we want people to know things: when a march is happening, that the police are beating people, that the white house welcomes white supremacists

comes up against doxxing - publishing home addresses and contact information for harassment super common on twitter and pushes people away from it.

case study: a person want to organize their work environment - they want people involved to know and want to be known, want to be accessible to people with questions - do they want their information spread on social media without context as an "agitator" - will they be able to work again?

tools

general privacy basics

vs. people with access to your computer (family/friends/roommates)

  • Incognito/Private browsing/clear history on close
  • different passwords for every account
  • unique, long, memorable
  • "memorable" usually means using a password manager to remember it for you (LastPass or KeePass)
  • don't let your browser remember your passwords
  • SnapChat or WhatsApp delete messages right away

privacy from employers

don't use a work computer for your communication

  • traffic is easily tracked even if you are using incognito mode/private browsing - IP addresses show up places (twitterbots like https://twitter.com/gccaedits )
  • use https to encrypt so your passwords and info aren't being transmitted in cleartext

privacy from corporations

really does the convenience vs privacy thing here. advertising and tracking is ubiquitous, so some of these practices will stop websites from working the way they want to.

  • use an ad-blocker uBlock Origin
  • use Privacy Badger from eff.org
  • use https everywhere
  • duckduckgo or startpage for searching

  • encrypted cloud storage like Sync.com

  • turn off all your location tracking data on your phone, especially history
